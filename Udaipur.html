UDAYPUR

Udaipur

Often referred to as the "Venice of the East," Udaipur is a city of lakes, palaces, and rich heritage. Nestled beside the picturesque Lake Pichola, Udaipur offers a unique blend of natural beauty and royal architecture.

Best Time to Visit Udaipur

Udaipur experiences a desert climate. Summers can be quite hot, while the monsoon season brings a mystical charm to the city. The winter months (October to March) are the best time to visit, with pleasant days and cool evenings.

Places to Visit in Udaipur

City Palace

Built in 1559 on the banks of Lake Pichola, the City Palace is a blend of Chinese, medieval, and European architectural styles. The palace complex houses 11 smaller palaces, each built during different reigns.

Lake Pichola

Extended by Rana Udai Singh II in the 16th century, Lake Pichola is surrounded by bathing ghats, palaces, and lush hills. Visitors can take boat rides to explore the lake and its islands.

Lake Palace

Located on Jag Niwas Island in Lake Pichola, this architectural marvel was built in 1746 and is now a luxury hotel. It has been featured in several Bollywood and Hollywood films.

Things to Do in Udaipur

Boat Ride

A boat ride on Lake Pichola during sunset offers a breathtaking view of the city's beauty.

Photography

Visit Saheliyon Ki Bari, a historic garden with fountains, marble elephants, and lush greenery, perfect for photography.

Ropeway Ride

The ropeway to Karni Mata Temple offers a stunning aerial view of Udaipur.

How to Reach Udaipur

Udaipur is well-connected by air, rail, and road:

  • By Air: The Maharana Pratap Airport is 22 km from the city center.
  • By Train: Udaipur has its own railway station with connections to major cities.
  • By Road: Udaipur is well-connected by national highways. Ahmedabad is 251 km away, while Delhi is 698 km away.
