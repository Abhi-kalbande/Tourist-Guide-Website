TAJ MAHAL

Taj Mahal

The history of Agra is rich and reflects the contributions of various rulers. However, it is most famously associated with the Mughal era. The Mughals, known for their architectural marvels, left behind a legacy of breathtaking monuments, with the Taj Mahal being the crown jewel.

Places to Visit in Agra

Agra is home to several historical landmarks that showcase the brilliance of Mughal architecture. Here are the top places to visit:

Taj Mahal

One of the Seven Wonders of the World, the Taj Mahal was commissioned by Emperor Shah Jahan in memory of his beloved wife, Mumtaz Mahal. This white marble mausoleum is a symbol of eternal love, adorned with intricate carvings and precious stones. It attracts millions of visitors every year.

Agra Fort

Built in the 16th century, the Red Fort of Agra is an outstanding example of Mughal architecture. This UNESCO World Heritage Site served as the main residence of the Mughal emperors and offers a stunning view of the Taj Mahal from its walls.

Fatehpur Sikri

Located 39 km west of Agra, Fatehpur Sikri was built by Emperor Akbar as his imperial capital. This abandoned city is home to several stunning monuments, including the Buland Darwaza, Panch Mahal, and the Tomb of Salim Chishti.

Sikandra

The tomb of Emperor Akbar, located in Sikandra, is a magnificent blend of Hindu, Christian, Islamic, Buddhist, and Jain architectural styles. The surrounding gardens add to its serene beauty.

Itmad-ud-Daulah's Tomb (Baby Taj)

Often called the "Baby Taj," this tomb was built by Nur Jahan, the wife of Emperor Jahangir, for her father. It is considered a precursor to the Taj Mahal and is renowned for its intricate marble inlay work.

Mehtab Bagh

A beautiful garden complex located across the Yamuna River, Mehtab Bagh provides a stunning view of the Taj Mahal, especially at sunset.
