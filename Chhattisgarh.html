CHHATTISGARH

Discover Chhattisgarh

Chhattisgarh, located in central India, is endowed with a rich cultural heritage and breathtaking natural beauty. The state is home to stunning waterfalls, lush forests, and ancient temples that make it a hidden gem for travelers.

Must-See Waterfalls

Chhattisgarh is often referred to as the 'Land of Waterfalls.' Some of the most beautiful waterfalls include:

  • Chitrakote Falls - Known as the "Niagara Falls of India," this waterfall cascades from a height of 100 feet.
  • Tirathgarh Falls - A mesmerizing 300-feet high waterfall located in the Kanger Valley National Park.
  • Amrit Dhara Falls - A scenic waterfall in the Koriya district, best visited during the monsoon season.
  • Mandawa Falls - A lesser-known but stunning waterfall surrounded by lush greenery.

Historical Significance

Chhattisgarh has a rich and diverse history, dating back to ancient times. The region was ruled by several powerful dynasties, including the Mauryas, Satvahanas, and Kalchuris. Later, it came under the control of the Marathas, Mughals, and British rule before achieving statehood in 2000.

Best Time to Visit

The ideal time to visit Chhattisgarh depends on your preference:

  • Winter (October - February): Pleasant weather, perfect for sightseeing.
  • Monsoon (July - September): Lush greenery and full-flowing waterfalls, though some areas may be difficult to access.
  • Summer (March - June): Hot and dry, not ideal for travel.

Top Tourist Attractions

  • Bastar: Famous for its tribal culture, Chitrakote Falls, and Kanger Valley National Park.
  • Raipur: The capital city, known for its museums, temples, and local markets.
  • Bilaspur: Home to Ratanpur Fort and the ancient Malhar archaeological site.
  • Jagdalpur: Rich in cultural heritage, with Bastar Palace and scenic caves.
  • Bhilai: Known for its steel industry and the famous Maitri Bagh Zoo.
  • Rajim: Famous for its ancient temples and the Rajim Kumbh Mela.

Things to Do in Chhattisgarh

  • Witness the spectacular Chitrakote Falls.
  • Explore the dense forests of Kanger Valley National Park.
  • Visit the historic Bhilai Steel Plant.
  • Discover the unique tribal culture of Bastar.
  • Enjoy a wildlife safari in Barnawapara Wildlife Sanctuary.
  • Attend the vibrant Bastar Dussehra festival.
